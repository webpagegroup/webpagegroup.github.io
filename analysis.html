Quantative Economy

This section will provide an overview of the analysis. The project report and code can be downloaded from the Github Github repository.

Based on requirement of stay nights, Airbnb could be divided into minimum nights of more than 30 nights and less than 30 nights. Comparing the different relationship between average housing price and the density of these two kinds Airbnb.

 Over 30 nights: the minimum stay night is over 30 nights, which can provide survey for people who need to stay more than one month.
 The r square is 0.2664, which means that the result of this regression can explain 26.6% of the observation.
 The estimate of this of this equation is 1545, which means the density of this kind of Airbnb is correlative with housing price.

 Less 30 nights: the minimum stay night is less 30 nights, which can provide more convenience for people who does not need to stay for long time, such as tourism.
 The R Square is 0.211, which means that the result of this regression can explain 21.1% of the observation.
 The estimate of this of this equation is 180, which means the density of this kind of Airbnb is correlative with housing price.

Geospatial Distribution

Ripley’s K function is used for further exploration of the distribution characteristics. Figure shows the distance on the horizontal axis and the value of the L function on the vertical axis. If the specific distance L(d) is larger than the expected value (blue line), the clustering degree of the distribution is higher than that of the random distribution of the distance (analysis scale). The observation value is larger than the upper limit of the confidence interval, the spatial clustering of the distance has statistical significance. The graph shows that the L(d) value is much greater than expected, which indicates that both Airbnb and Propertys are clustered in the urban area and that there is no evidence of random or uniform distribution in this scale.

Standard deviational ellipse can understand the scattering range and direction of data. This method is widely used in the study of virus transmission and air pollution diffusion. Figure shows that the main expansion axes (the longer axis of the eclipse) are concentrated in the direction of Queens and Long Island, while the expansion of Bronx is not as pronounced.

Limitations

- Ignore the data of other years: changes of Airbnb and housing price
-Rough model: other impacts and the natural fluctuations of houseing prices A small R-square: low model reliability
-Fragmentation analysis: less combination between Spatial and quantitative analysis Focusing on New York: unable to be applied to other cities
