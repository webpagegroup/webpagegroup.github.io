Part I: Quantative Economy

The quantitative analysis mainly focuses on the effect of Airbnb density on average housing price on neighbourhood level. We divide Airbnb into different types based on several obvious features, to conduct a more accurate analysis. As two obvious attributes of Airbnb, minimum stay night and the type of Airbnb are chosen to further refine the analysis. Airbnb with different minimum stay night represents different target tenement, which would be introduced first.


Minimum Stay Nights

The rent of Airbnb can be divided into short-term and long-term rent. Airbnb already has a landing page dedicated to long-term rentals and sublets that over 28 days to six months. This part gives the relationship between average housing price and the density of two types of Airbnb.

Based on requirement of stayed nights, Airbnb could be divided into minimum nights of more than 28 nights and less than it. Comparing the different relationship between average housing price and the density of these two kinds Airbnb.

Over 28 minimum nights Airbnb:

 This kind of Airbnb requires over 28 minimum stayed nights, which can provide service for long-term stayed people.

 The R-Square means that the result of this regression can explain about 26.6% of the observation.

 The estimate of this equation shows that the density of this kind of Airbnb is positive correlated with housing price, and it can affect housing price efficiently.


Less 28 minimum nights Airbnb:

 This kind of Airbnb requires less 28 minimum stayed nights, which can provide services for short-term stayed people, such as tourism.

 The R-Square means that the result of this regression can explain 21.1% of the observation.

 The slope of this equation is lower than that of the other type of Airbnb, which means that this part of Airbnb can not affect housing price as efficient as the other one.


Type of Airbnb

Since their regression graphs are similar and cannot directly show what they mean directly, so we have omitted them here.

The shared room, as the cheapest and most rare one among these three types, the guests should share an entire house with other people or homeowner. The chart shows the R-squares and the K of all three types. As figure shows, the shared room has a low R-square, which even may not informative. However, it has a very high K. Therefore, the shared room has little impact on housing price, but once it works, it would make a huge effect.

Secondly, the private part would give the guests their own bedrooms, while other space is shared. The private part has a relatively stable data, shows that about 19.5% of observations match the equation.

Entire place is the most popular type, accounting for 52.39% of Airbnb in New York. Meanwhile, it has a high R-square, which means it is actually more corelative with housing price. It is obvious that the type of entire place Airbnb has a more stable and stronger relationship with housing price.


Part II: Geospatial Distribution

Ripley’s K

Ripley’s K function is used for further exploration of the distribution characteristics.(See detials in Report) Figure shows the distance on the horizontal axis and the value of the L function on the vertical axis. If the specific distance L(d) is larger than the expected value (blue line), the clustering degree of the distribution is higher than that of the random distribution of the distance (analysis scale). The observation value is larger than the upper limit of the confidence interval, the spatial clustering of the distance has statistical significance. The graph shows that the L(d) value is much greater than expected, which indicates that both Airbnb and Propertys are clustered in the urban area and that there is no evidence of random or uniform distribution in this scale.

Standard Deviation Ellipse

Standard deviational ellipse can understand the scattering range and direction of data. This method is widely used in the study of virus transmission and air pollution diffusion. Figure shows that the main expansion axes (the longer axis of the eclipse) are concentrated in the direction of Queens and Long Island, while the expansion of Bronx is not as pronounced.


Part III: Limitations

 We only do a horizontal relationship between Airbnb and housing prices without considering their relationship in the vertical level.

 We tacitly think that all the observations in linear regressions that do not match the equation are not affecting the house price or have less impact, but in fact it may not be.

 The R-squares in our regressions are relatively small, lack of reliability.

 For geographic analysis, we just predict the plan and development of Airbnb and housing on a geographical level, without carefully thinking about constraints resulting from the planning and policy of New York City.

 Our analysis is fragmentary, unable to combine Spatial and quantitative analysis
